**Central bet being proven:** Kids learn hockey IQ faster when the ice is the interface, not the textbook.

## The shape of the 30 days

Four weeks, one dominant goal each.

| Week | Days | Dominant Goal |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1–7 | Ship MVP — rink feature live, 20 scenarios, tier gate working |
| 2 | 8–14 | Get users — 20 real kids/parents using it, structured feedback captured |
| 3 | 15–21 | Iterate on signal — fix what broke, grow content, deepen the product |
| 4 | 22–30 | Convert and compound — first paying customers, first case study, plan v1.1 |

## Week 1 — Build and ship (Days 1–7)

**Dominant goal:** ice-iq.vercel.app serves rink scenarios to real users by Sunday night.

- Days 1–2 · Integration: Rink.jsx + tier gating + progress wiring
- Days 3–4 · Scenarios: 20 seeded, tone/terminology reviewed
- Day 5 · Progress and polish: tier gating verified, every existing question type still works
- Day 6 · Mobile + ship: real-device test, ship with documented minor issues
- Day 7 · Rest

## Week 2 — Distribution (Days 8–14)

**Dominant goal:** 20 real people use the app and give structured feedback.

- Day 8 · Feedback infra: exit poll, Cal.com link, feedback log
- Days 9–11 · Outreach: minor-hockey families, coaches, TikTok
- Day 12 · Watch people use it (in-person where possible)
- Day 13 · Synthesize top 3 patterns
- Day 14 · Decide exactly 3 changes for week 3

## Week 3 — Iterate on signal (Days 15–21)

**Dominant goal:** Build what users told you they need. Grow the scenario library.

- Days 15–17 · Ship the 3 priority fixes (≤3 days)
- Days 18–19 · Double the library (20 → 40 scenarios)
- Day 20 · Concept category filter (Pro tier justification)
- Day 21 · Second round of user testing

## Week 4 — Convert and compound (Days 22–30)

**Dominant goal:** First paying customers. First case study.

- Day 22 · Verify Stripe end-to-end
- Days 23–25 · Convert warm leads to Pro/Family/Team tier (target 3-4)
- Day 26 · Build the case study
- Day 27 · Document what worked
- Day 28 · Plan next 30 days based on actual data
- Day 29 · One big public swing
- Day 30 · Rest and review

## Honest risks

1. **You'll build instead of distribute.** Most technical founders do this.
2. **BlueChip will pull attention.** Block IceIQ time on the calendar explicitly.
3. **You'll listen to the wrong feedback.** Pain and drop-off are signals; pleasure is politeness.
4. **You'll scope-creep into a platform.** Through month 1, ship scenarios and small fixes, not big features.

## Meta-observation

> Your background is unusually well-suited to this specific product. Post-secondary teaching, youth coaching, systems thinking, HR — the intersection of "knows how kids learn," "knows hockey at a developmental level," and "can build" is thin.
>
> **IceIQ is defensible because you're defensible.**
>
> The product side will be fine. The distribution side is your growth edge.
